Discover Sacred Anuradhapura

Explore 25+ magnificent historical and spiritual sites just minutes from Ancient Villa

Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi Tree

The most sacred Buddhist site in Sri Lanka, this 2,300-year-old Bodhi tree is believed to be grown from a cutting of the original tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment.

Ruwanwelisaya Stupa

A magnificent white stupa built by King Dutugemunu in 140 BC, considered one of the most sacred Buddhist monuments.

Jetawanaramaya

Once the tallest stupa in the world at 120 meters, this 3rd-century monument showcases the architectural mastery of ancient Sri Lankan builders and remains an engineering marvel.

Isurumuniya Temple

Famous for its ancient rock carvings including the "Isurumuniya Lovers," this temple combines natural rock formations with intricate Buddhist art dating back to the 3rd century BC.

The Star Gate of Ancient Anuradhapura

A mystical ancient structure believed to align with celestial events, this gate reflects the astronomical knowledge of ancient Sri Lankan architects from centuries past.

Vessagiriya Buddhist Forest Monastery

A serene forest monastery with ancient rock caves and meditation sites, offering a glimpse into the monastic life of Buddhist monks from the 2nd century BC.

Samadhi Buddha Statue

This serene 4th-century limestone statue depicts Buddha in deep meditation. Its peaceful expression has inspired countless devotees and is considered one of the finest Buddha statues in Sri Lanka.

Abhayagiri Stupa

Center of a vast monastic complex that once housed 5,000 monks. This 2nd century BC monument represents the height of ancient Sri Lankan Buddhist architecture and scholarship.

Sandakada Pahana (Moonstone)

Intricately carved semi-circular stone slabs that guard temple entrances, representing the Buddhist concept of Samsara. These artistic masterpieces showcase ancient Sri Lankan craftsmanship.

Eth Pokuna (Elephant Pond)

A massive ancient reservoir built to supply water to the Abhayagiri Monastery, this pond exemplifies the sophisticated hydraulic engineering of ancient Anuradhapura.

Lankarama Stupa

A beautifully preserved stupa from the 1st century BC, surrounded by stone pillars, offering a tranquil setting for reflection and historical exploration.

Abhayagiriya Museum

A repository of artifacts from the Abhayagiri Monastery, this museum showcases ancient sculptures, inscriptions, and relics dating back to the 1st century BC.

Thuparamaya Dagoba

The oldest stupa in Sri Lanka, built in the 3rd century BC, it houses the collarbone relic of the Buddha and is a cornerstone of Buddhist heritage.

Kuttam Pokuna (Twin Ponds)

Masterpiece of ancient hydraulic engineering, these beautifully designed bathing pools for monks showcase the sophisticated water management systems of ancient Anuradhapura.

Aukana Buddha Statue

A towering 5th-century standing Buddha statue carved from a single rock, this masterpiece stands as a testament to the sculptural skill of ancient Sri Lankan artisans.

Tissa Wewa Reservoir

Ancient man-made lake built by King Devanampiyatissa in 3rd century BC. This engineering marvel still supplies water today and offers breathtaking sunset views over the sacred city.

Jethawanarama Image House (Palimage)

An ancient image house near the Jetawanaramaya stupa, housing significant Buddha statues and reflecting the artistic traditions of the 3rd century BC.

Haththikuchchi Temple

A lesser-known temple with ancient ruins and a peaceful ambiance, reflecting the spiritual life of monks from the 3rd century BC.
